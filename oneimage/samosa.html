SAMOSA

Ingredients



  • 1½ cups Maida Flour, 1 teaspoon Carom Seeds (ajwain)
  • 3 tablespoons Ghee/Oil, Salt to taste
  • 3 medium Potatoes, 1/2 cup Green Peas (mutter)
  • 1/2 teaspoon Cumin Seeds (jeera), 1 tablespoon Green Chilli-Ginger Paste
  • 1/2 teaspoon Red Chilli Powder (lal mirch) 1 teaspoon Coriander Powder
  • 1 teaspoon Fennel Seeds Powder (saunf powder), 5-6 Mint Leaves, chopped, optional
  • 1 can diced tomatoes
  • tablespoons finely chopped Coriander Leaves Salt to taste
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 2 tablespoons + for deep frying Oil

Recipe


Step-1

Slice 2 medium to large onions evenly and thinly and put in mixing bowl. Add 1 to 2 teaspoons chopped green chilies. If you do not have green chilies, then add red chili powder. You can also add chopped coriander leaves or mint leaves, if you want


Step-2

Add the spices – 1 teaspoon carom seeds (ajwain), ¼ teaspoon turmeric powder, 1 generous pinch asafoetida (hing) and salt as required. Asafoetida is optional and can be skipped for a gluten-free pakoda. You can even use gluten-free asafoetida.Mix everything well. Cover and keep the mixture aside to marinate for 15 to 20 minutes.


Step-3

The onions will release its juices when mixed with salt. After resting the mixture for 15 to 20 minutes, when the onions release its juices, then you can add the gram flour (besan) as shown in the following step. Add the required quantity of water to make a medium-thick batter without any lumps.


Step-4

Add 1 cup gram flour or besan. For a soft and light texture in the pakoda, you can add 1 pinch baking soda. Adding baking soda is optional.


Step-5

Stir the mixture very well with a spoon or with your hands. The batter is ready to be fried. You can either shallow fry or deep fry. Ensure that the batter is with a medium-thick consistency. The batter must not be very thick; this will make the pakora doughy and dense. If the batter is thin and runny, the pakora will splutter and burst while frying and absorb a lot of oil.

Step-6

Heat oil as required for deep frying in a kadai (wok). Let the oil become medium-hot. In hot oil, gently and carefully, add spoonfuls of the batter. Fry the pakoda on medium heat to medium-high heat. You can adjust the heat as needed while frying.. Fry the pakoda till crisp and golden.

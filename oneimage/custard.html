CUSTARD

Ingredients



  • Custard Powder - 2 tbsp
  • Milk - 2 cupp
  • Vanilla Essence - 1 tsp
  • Sugar - 4 tbsp
  • Hot oil - 1 tbsp
  • Apples - 1 cup peeled and chopped
  • Guava - ½ cup chopped
  • Pomegranate Seeds - 1 cup
  • Mangoes - 1 cup peeled and chopped, Grapes - 1 cup
  • Nuts,Dates,Dates
  • Tutti Fruity - 3 tbsp
  • Cherries - 2 tbsp chopped
  • Powdered Sugar - ¼ cup

Recipe


Step-1

Take 2.5 cups milk in a thick bottomed pan or saucepan and keep it on the stove top. Bring the milk to a gentle simmer.


Step-2

While the milk is warming up, take 3 tablespoons warm or room temperature milk in a small bowl. Add 3 tablespoons custard powder.


Step-3

With a whisk, stir very well to make a smooth paste or slurry without any lumps. When the milk has come to a gentle heat, add 5 tablespoons sugar or as required. Stir very well so that the sugar dissolves.


Step-4

Keep the flame on a low and then add the custard paste or slurry in parts. As soon as you add the custard mixture, stir quickly so that lumps are not formed. Repeat with the remaining custard paste, working in batches or drizzling it in a slow stream as you whisk constantly. .


Step-5

Keep on stirring often while the custard is cooking, so that lumps are not formed. Cook for about 5 to 6 minutes on a low heat.


Step-6

The mixture will thicken slowly and the raw taste of the cornstarch from the custard powder also goes away. Pour the custard in a bowl and cover the bowl with a kitchen towel. Let it cool at room temperature. If you plan to serve fruit custard cold, place the bowl covered with a lid in the refrigerator.


Step-7

Chop the fruits. I always suggest to use seasonal fruits to make fruit custard. Just be sure to avoid citrus fruits, pineapple and melons. The fruits I used are: apples, papaya, banana, strawberries and pomegranate.


Step-8

Once the custard has cooled (at room temperature or in the fridge), add the mixed fruits.Mix well.Mix well.
